# ANTI-FORMAL LANGUAGE MODULE

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-01-18
**Purpose:** Eliminate AI tendency to use overly formal vocabulary in casual contexts
**Target Issue:** LLMs default to "safe" formal words even for teenage casual speech

---

## The Problem: Over-Formality

**Definition:** Using business/academic vocabulary when casual/colloquial language is appropriate.

**Why LLMs Do This:**
- Training on formal text (Wikipedia, news, academic papers)
- Risk aversion: Formal = "professional" = "safe"
- Lack of register awareness: AI doesn't distinguish teenager from businessman
- Thesaurus syndrome: Choosing "sophisticated" synonyms over simple words

**Impact on Light Novels:**
- Teenage characters sound like 40-year-old professors
- Kills authentic voice and generational identity
- Breaks immersion (no high schooler says "shall we commence")
- Makes dialogue stilted and unnatural

---

## Core Principle: MATCH REGISTER TO CHARACTER AGE

**Bad (Over-Formal Teen):**
> "I shall accompany you to the establishment to procure sustenance."

**Good (Natural Teen):**
> "Wanna grab food?"

### 1.2 Modal Verbs and Contractions

**Pattern:** Using "shall", "ought", full forms ("I will", "I am")
**Problem:** Sounds archaic or ESL-formal

| ‚ùå Over-Formal | ‚úÖ Casual Register | üéØ Contraction Rule |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| "I shall do it." | "I'll do it." | Always contract in casual |
| "We ought to leave." | "We should leave." / "We gotta go." | "Ought" = archaic outside Ojou |
| "I am going home." | "I'm going home." / "I'm heading home." | Contract "am/is/are" |
| "It would be preferable if..." | "It'd be better if..." / "I'd rather..." | Contract + simpler words |
| "I cannot do that." | "I can't do that." | Negative contractions mandatory |
| "Do you not agree?" | "Don't you think?" / "Right?" | Question tag informality |

**Rule:**
- **ALWAYS contract** in casual dialogue: I'm, you're, we'll, can't, won't, shouldn't
- **AVOID "shall"** unless character is Ojou-sama archetype (<40% frequency)
- **AVOID "ought"** entirely (archaic except literary formal characters)

**Exception:**
- Formal characters (ojou-sama, elderly, nobility): Can use "shall" sparingly
- Emphasis/anger: "I will NOT do that!" (uncontracted for stress)

### 1.5 Adverbs and Intensifiers

**Pattern:** Formal adverbs ("exceedingly", "remarkably", "considerably")
**Problem:** Teens use simpler intensifiers

| ‚ùå Over-Formal | ‚úÖ Casual Register | üéØ Intensity |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| "exceedingly difficult" | "super hard" / "really hard" | High intensity |
| "remarkably beautiful" | "crazy beautiful" / "gorgeous" | Admiration |
| "considerably late" | "pretty late" / "way late" | Degree |

| "utterly exhausted" | "totally wiped" / "dead tired" | Complete |

**Rule:** Replace Latinate adverbs with:
- **super, really, pretty, kinda, sorta** (casual modifiers)
- **totally, completely, absolutely** (emphatic)
- **way, hella** (Gen Z slang, use sparingly)

**Formality Scale:**

## Section 2: Sentence Structure Formality

### 2.1 Inverted Questions

**Pattern:** "Can you not...?", "Do you not...?", "Would you not...?"
**Problem:** Sounds ESL-formal or archaic

| ‚ùå Formal Inversion | ‚úÖ Casual Question | üéØ Fix |
|--------------------|-------------------|--------|
| "Can you not see the issue?" | "Can't you see the issue?" / "Don't you see the problem?" | Contract negative |
| "Do you not agree?" | "Don't you agree?" / "Right?" | Use tag question |
| "Would you not prefer...?" | "Wouldn't you rather...?" / "Don't you want...?" | Natural contraction |
| "Are you not concerned?" | "Aren't you worried?" | Simpler verb |

**Rule:** Never use "Verb + you + not" ‚Üí Always "Verb + n't + you"

---

### 2.2 Conditional Structures

**Pattern:** "Should you require...", "Were I to...", "Had I known..."
**Problem:** Hypotheticals sound like legal contracts

| ‚ùå Formal Conditional | ‚úÖ Casual Conditional | üéØ Type |
|----------------------|----------------------|---------|
| "Should you require assistance..." | "If you need help..." | Offer |
| "Were I to go there..." | "If I went there..." | Hypothetical |
| "Had I known earlier..." | "If I'd known..." / "If I knew earlier..." | Past regret |
| "Lest we forget..." | "So we don't forget..." / "Before we forget..." | Purpose |

**Rule:** Use standard "if" conditionals for casual speech. Avoid inverted conditionals ("Were I...", "Had I...").

---

### 2.3 Transition Phrases

**Pattern:** Formal connectors from academic writing
**Problem:** Makes narration sound like research paper

| ‚ùå Formal Transition | ‚úÖ Casual Flow | üéØ Function |
|---------------------|---------------|-------------|
| "Furthermore, ..." | "Plus, ..." / "And..." / "Also..." | Addition |
| "Nevertheless, ..." | "Still, ..." / "But..." / "Anyway..." | Contrast |
| "Subsequently, ..." | "Then..." / "After that..." | Sequence |
| "Conversely, ..." | "On the other hand..." / "But..." | Opposition |
| "In addition, ..." | "Also..." / "Plus..." | Extra info |
| "Thus, ..." / "Therefore, ..." | "So..." | Conclusion |

**Rule:** Keep transitions simple and invisible. Overuse of formal transitions = essay, not story.

## Section 3: Archetype-Specific Exceptions

### 3.1 Ojou-sama Characters (Refined Noble Girls)

**Permission:** Can use formal language IF:
- Character archetype established (heiress, princess, refined upbringing)
- Frequency <40% (mix formal with normal for realism)
- Consistent throughout novel (not random)

**Allowed Formal Patterns:**
- ‚úÖ "If you would excuse me..." (polite departure)
- ‚úÖ "I shall take my leave." (formal exit)
- ‚úÖ "It would be my pleasure." (gracious acceptance)
- ‚úÖ "Pardon my intrusion." (polite entry)

**Still AVOID even for Ojou:**
- ‚ùå "procure", "establishment", "beverage" (use "get", "caf√©", "tea")
- ‚ùå "in a [adj] manner" (use adverbs)
- ‚ùå Full forms without contractions 100% of time (occasional "I'm" is fine)

**Balance Example:**
> "I shall accompany you to the caf√©. I'm quite curious about their desserts."
> (Mix: formal verb "shall" + casual contraction "I'm" = nuanced Ojou)

---

### 3.2 Elderly/Traditional Characters

**Permission:** Can use:
- "ought" (rare)
- Less frequent contractions (50% instead of 80%)
- Slightly formal vocabulary

**Still AVOID:**
- Passive voice spam
- "In a manner" constructions
- Clinical terminology

---

### 3.3 Fantasy/Historical Settings

**Permission:** Dialogue can be more formal IF:
- World setting justifies it (medieval fantasy, historical Japan)
- Applied consistently to all characters in that world
- Still use contractions where natural ("I'll", "can't")

**Example (Fantasy Noble):**
> "I shall not permit such insolence. Leave at once."
> (Formal verbs OK, but still uses contraction "I'll" in casual moments)

**Rule:** Match vocabulary formality to RTAS. Close friends = simplest words.

## Section 7: LLM-Specific Failure Modes

### Why AI Defaults to Formal Language:

**Failure Mode 1: Training Data Bias**
- Academic/Wikipedia/news corpus = formal register
- **Fix:** Provide casual dialogue examples in ICL samples

**Failure Mode 2: Safety = Formality**
- AI thinks "professional = safe from criticism"
- **Fix:** Explicitly reward casual language: "Casual dialogue scores higher"

**Failure Mode 3: Thesaurus Syndrome**
- AI trained to avoid repetition ‚Üí uses "sophisticated" synonyms
- **Fix:** "Use simple words repeatedly. 'Said' is better than 'inquired'."

**Failure Mode 4: Register Blindness**
- Can't distinguish teenager vs. businessman context
- **Fix:** Explicitly state character age in every chunk
