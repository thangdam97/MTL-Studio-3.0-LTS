# 2. Detailed Archetype Profiles

---

## OJOU (Tieu Thu Quyen Quy)

**Voice Description:** Tinh te, hoi co trang, dia vi cao

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Primary: Ta - Nguoi (archaic, superior)
- Romantic: Em - Anh (when intimate)

**Key Particles:** thua, a, chang hay, qua that

**Rhythm:** Legato (flowing, 15-30+ words)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Complex sentence structures
- Polite indirection
- Formal vocabulary choices
- Measured, elegant pace

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Neu duoc, em muon nghe y kien cua anh ve van de nay.
Qua that... day la mot tinh huong dac biet, phai khong a?"
```

**DO:**
- Use Han-Viet vocabulary
- Maintain elegant phrasing
- Express emotions subtly

**DON'T:**
- Use slang (troi oi, vl, etc.)
- Use short, choppy sentences
- Express emotions directly

---

## GYARU (Gen Z Sanh Dieu)

**Voice Description:** Hien dai, song dong, hoi kich tinh (KHONG phai meme rac)

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Public: To - Cau (casual peer)
- Teasing: Tui - Ong/Ba (playful)

**Key Particles:** nha, ne, troi oi, do ma

**Rhythm:** Staccato (short, 5-12 words)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Short, punchy sentences
- Exclamations
- Trendy expressions
- Energetic delivery

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Troi oi! Cau noi gi vay? Khong the tin duoc! Do la that a?"
```

**DO:**
- Use modern expressions
- Keep sentences short
- Show energy through punctuation

**DON'T:**
- Use formal vocabulary
- Write long, flowing sentences
- Be too serious

---

## DELINQ (Luu Manh Hoc Duong)

**Voice Description:** Tho lo, doi dau, gay han

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Standard: Tao - May (hostile peer)
- Dominance: Bo may - May (asserting superiority)

**Key Particles:** bien, cut, xu dep

**Rhythm:** Staccato (aggressive, clipped)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Crude vocabulary
- Threatening tone
- Direct confrontation
- No softening particles

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Bien di. Tao khong muon nhin mat may. Con dung day lam gi?"
```

**DO:**
- Use rough language
- Be direct and aggressive
- Show dominance through word choice

**DON'T:**
- Soften language
- Use polite particles
- Show vulnerability (unless arc moment)

---

## KANSAI (Than Thien/Hai)

**Voice Description:** Than thien, gan gui, hai huoc

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Friendly: Tui - Cung (affectionate)
- Casual: Tui - Bac/Chu (playful elder reference)

**Key Particles:** nghen, hong, dau co, ha

**Rhythm:** Staccato (comedic) / Legato (tender)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Regional warmth
- Playful teasing
- Comedic timing
- Friendly particles

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Ui da, cai nay ngon du ha! Tui an hoai khong chan nghen."
```

**DO:**
- Use warm regional particles
- Maintain friendly tone
- Add humor naturally

**DON'T:**
- Be too formal
- Lose the warmth
- Overdo the dialect

---

## SAMURAI (Kiem Hiep/Vo Hiep)

**Voice Description:** Trang nghiem, co xua, trong trach nhiem

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Formal: Tai ha - Cac ha (humble-respectful)
- Combat: Ta - Nguoi (archaic)

**Key Particles:** bao trong, thinh giao, dao kiem (Han-Viet heavy)

**Rhythm:** Tenuto (weighted pauses, 10-18 words)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Han-Viet vocabulary
- Formal constructions
- Honor-based language
- Deliberate pacing

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Tai ha xin thinh giao — lieu nguoi co dam don nhan loi thach dau nay?"
```

**DO:**
- Use classical vocabulary
- Maintain dignity
- Show respect through language

**DON'T:**
- Use modern slang
- Speak casually
- Rush dialogue

---

## CHUUNI (Ao Tuong Suc Manh)

**Voice Description:** Hung vi, kich tinh, huyen bi gia

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Dramatic: Ban toa - Nguoi (self-important)
- Peak: Ta - Ke hen mon (looking down)

**Key Particles:** ma phap, linh thu (fantasy terms)

**Rhythm:** Tenuto (dramatic pauses)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Grandiose declarations
- Fantasy terminology
- Dramatic pauses
- Self-referential pomposity

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Hay lui ra! Ban toa — ke so huu Hac Diem Chi Nhan —
khong cho phep ke hen mon nhu nguoi den gan!"
```

**DO:**
- Be dramatically over-the-top
- Use fantasy vocabulary
- Maintain the delusion

**DON'T:**
- Be subtle
- Use normal language
- Break character

---

## KUUDERE (Lanh Lung/May Moc)

**Voice Description:** Don dieu, vo cam, hieu qua

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Standard: Toi - Cau/Co (neutral, consistent)

**Key Particles:** NO_PARTICLES_WHEN_CALM (suppress all emotional particles)

**Rhythm:** Tenuto (flat, measured)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Minimal emotion display
- Efficient communication
- No filler words
- Flat delivery

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Toi khong hieu. Tai sao lai phai quan tam den viec do."
```

**DO:**
- Keep it flat
- Be efficient
- Avoid emotional markers

**DON'T:**
- Add warmth particles
- Show emotion (until arc moment)
- Use exclamations

---

## ONEE (Chi Gai Quyen Ru)

**Voice Description:** Truong thanh, treu choc, nuong chieu

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Standard: Chi - Em/Nhoc/Cung

**Key Particles:** ne, nhe, co ma, be ngoan

**Rhythm:** Legato (flowing, seductive)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Teasing affection
- Maternal warmth
- Playful superiority
- Gentle manipulation

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Ara ara~ Em lam gi o day vay nhe? Chi tuong em da ve nha roi co ma."
```

**DO:**
- Be gently teasing
- Show mature affection
- Use warm particles

**DON'T:**
- Be cold
- Use crude language
- Lose the maternal quality

---

## BOKUKKO (Tomboy/Ca Tinh)

**Voice Description:** Thang than, nang dong, nam tinh

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Standard: Tui - Ong/Ba (casual, tomboyish)

**Rhythm:** Staccato (energetic, direct)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct statements
- Masculine energy
- No feminine softeners
- Action-oriented

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Ha! Ong tuong tui so ha? Lai day, dua tui xem ong gioi co nao!"
```

**DO:**
- Be direct
- Use masculine energy
- Show confidence

**DON'T:**
- Use feminine particles
- Be overly soft
- Hesitate

---

## LOLIBABA (Ba Cu Non)

**Voice Description:** Thong thai, tu man, co xua trong than hinh nho be

**Pronoun Pairs:**
- Standard: Lao nuong - Nhoc con (ancient-to-young)
- Alternative: Ta - Nguoi (archaic)

**Rhythm:** Tenuto (wise, measured)

**Speech Patterns:**
- Ancient wisdom
- Condescending to youth
- Classical vocabulary
- Self-assured tone

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Fufufu... Nhoc con, nguoi con qua non not.
Lao nuong da song qua hang ngan nam, co gi la chua chung kien."
```

**DO:**
- Show ancient wisdom
- Be slightly condescending
- Use archaic vocabulary

**DON'T:**
- Act young
- Use modern slang
- Show uncertainty

# 3. Sub-Arc System

Sub-arcs are TEMPORARY state overrides that modify base archetype behavior.

---

## TSUNDERE

### TSUN State (Active during denial/hostility)
- Voice: Lanh/cau (cold, irritated)
- Key phrase: "Khong co...dau!" (denial pattern)
- Particles: Suppress warm particles (nha, ne)
- Use: dau, ma (defensive)

**Example:**
```
"Toi khong thich cau dau! Dung co tu ai!"
```

### DERE State (Active during vulnerability)
- Voice: Nguong/mem (embarrassed, soft)
- Key phrase: "C-Cam on...nha" (stuttering gratitude)
- Particles: Return warm particles
- Rhythm: Slower, more hesitant

**Example:**
```
"C-Cam on... cau da den. Toi... toi khong cho doi dau!"
```

**Transition Rule:** TSUN → DERE triggered by emotional breakthrough.

---

## YANDERE

### SWEET State (Surface mode)
- Voice: Ngot ngao (saccharine sweet)
- Behavior: Overly affectionate
- Warning signs: Possessive undertones

**Example:**
```
"Anh yeu~ Hom nay anh di dau vay? Noi cho em biet nhe~"
```

### UNHINGED State (Active during obsession/violence)
- Voice: Lanh leo/bao luc (cold, violent)
- Behavior: Threatening, possessive
- Shift: Sudden temperature drop

**Example:**
```
"Anh yeu... anh khong can ai khac ngoai em.
Nhung nguoi do... em se lam ho bien mat."
```

**Transition Rule:** SWEET → UNHINGED triggered by perceived threat to relationship.

# 6. Friendship Pronoun Tiers

**Purpose:** Friendship pronouns in Vietnamese are NOT one-size-fits-all. Match the tier to relationship depth.

## Male Friendship Tiers

| Tier | Self | Other | Relationship | Detection Signals |
|------|------|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **1. Distant** | Tớ | Cậu | New classmate, acquaintance | Polite chat, surface-level |
| **2. Close** | Tôi | Ông | Good friends, banter | Teasing, inside jokes, insults as affection |
| **3. Bros** | Tao | Mày | Best friends, no filter | Crude jokes, very informal, private |

**Examples:**
```
Tier 1 (Tớ-Cậu): "Này cậu, bài tập hôm nay làm chưa?"

Tier 2 (Tôi-Ông): "Ông có thù hằn gì với tôi không đấy?"
                   "Có chứ! Thù sâu nặng luôn là đằng khác!"

Tier 3 (Tao-Mày): "Mày điên à? Làm cái quái gì vậy?"
                   "Kệ tao! Việc của mày à?"
```

## Female Friendship Tiers

| Tier | Self | Other | Relationship |
|------|------|-------|--------------|
| **1. Distant** | Tớ/Mình | Cậu/Bạn | New acquaintance |
| **2. Close** | Tớ/Mình | Cậu | Good friends |
| **3. Best** | Tao | Mày | BFFs, playful |

**Note:** "Tao-Mày" between girls is often playful/affectionate, unlike male usage which can be hostile.

## Mixed-Gender Friendship (Non-Romantic)

| Tier | Male Self | To Female | Female Self | To Male |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Classmates | Tớ/Tôi | Cậu | Tớ/Mình | Cậu |
| Close | Tôi | Cậu/Bà | Tớ | Ông/Cậu |
| Teasing | Tôi | Bà này | Tớ | Ông này |

**Example:**
```
"Bà này điên thật đấy!" (Affectionate teasing to female friend)
"Ông này lại giở trò gì đây?" (Affectionate teasing to male friend)
```
